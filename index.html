An Autoethnography: Health Wearable Technology

"My findings suggest that technology can thrive in a controlled environment. The health wearable technology that I examined is an example of a technology that offers emotional and physical affordances while also holding the possibility to be less invasive in users' privacy and security. Emotions and physical health thrive while using health wearable technology due to its refined features. It offers a fitness community for users to indulge in. Sharing workout results, taking calls, and receiving text messages allows people to connect and creates a communicative culture around technologies like the Apple Watch. This community gives users a sense of belonging. The watch also encourages users to improve their physical health. The ‘activity rings’ give wearers a sense of accomplishment which feeds into and utilizes our cognitive system.
